11. Noise & Error Mitigation

Why Noise Matters

Real quantum computers operate in the NISQ era (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum). Current qubits are fragile — they interact with their environment, causing decoherence that degrades quantum information.

Types of Noise

  • Depolarizing noise: With probability p, a random Pauli error (X, Y, or Z) is applied. This uniformly mixes the state toward the maximally mixed state.
  • Dephasing (phase damping): Loss of phase information without energy loss. The off-diagonal elements of the density matrix decay.
  • Amplitude damping: Energy relaxation — the qubit decays from |1⟩ to |0⟩ over time (T1 decay).
  • Readout errors: The measurement device misidentifies |0⟩ as |1⟩ or vice versa.

Density Matrix Simulation

Noisy quantum states can't be represented by a single state vector. Instead, we use a density matrix ρ that captures both quantum and classical uncertainty. Goqu's density matrix simulator applies noise channels after each gate.

With no noise, a Bell state gives only |00⟩ and |11⟩. As noise increases, |01⟩ and |10⟩ appear — these are errors introduced by decoherence.

Error Mitigation

While full quantum error correction requires many physical qubits per logical qubit, error mitigation techniques can improve results on NISQ hardware:

  • Zero-Noise Extrapolation (ZNE): Run the circuit at multiple noise levels and extrapolate to zero noise
  • Measurement error mitigation: Calibrate readout errors and correct the counts
  • Dynamical decoupling: Insert identity-equivalent gate sequences to refocus errors
  • Probabilistic error cancellation (PEC): Decompose noisy operations into ideal operations with quasi-probability sampling

Goqu Code

// Create a noisy simulation
nm := noise.New()
nm.AddDefaultError(1, noise.Depolarizing1Q(0.05))
nm.AddDefaultError(2, noise.Depolarizing2Q(0.05))

sim := densitymatrix.New(2).WithNoise(nm)
counts, _ := sim.Run(circuit, 1024)

// Zero-Noise Extrapolation
res, _ := mitigation.RunZNE(ctx, mitigation.ZNEConfig{
    Circuit:      circuit,
    ScaleFactors: []float64{1, 3, 5},
    Extrapolator: mitigation.LinearExtrapolator,
})

Exercises

  1. At what noise level does the Bell state become effectively indistinguishable from random? (Hint: all four outcomes equally likely)
  2. Why does depolarizing noise drive the state toward the maximally mixed state (ρ = I/2ⁿ)?
  3. Current superconducting qubits have gate errors of ~0.1-1%. How many gates can you apply before noise dominates?
