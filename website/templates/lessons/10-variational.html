The Variational Approach

Many quantum algorithms designed for near-term (NISQ) hardware use a hybrid quantum-classical strategy:

  1. A parameterized quantum circuit (ansatz) prepares a state |ψ(θ)⟩
  2. The quantum computer measures an observable (e.g., energy)
  3. A classical optimizer adjusts the parameters θ to minimize the measured value
  4. Repeat until convergence

Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE)

VQE finds the ground state energy of a Hamiltonian H by minimizing ⟨ψ(θ)|H|ψ(θ)⟩. This is crucial for quantum chemistry — simulating molecules to discover new materials and drugs.

QAOA

The Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm tackles combinatorial problems like MaxCut. It alternates between a cost layer (encodes the problem) and a mixer layer (explores solutions), parameterized by angles γ and β.

Goqu Code

// VQE for a simple Hamiltonian
hamiltonian, _ := pauli.NewPauliSum([]pauli.PauliString{
    pauli.NewPauliString(-1.0, map[int]pauli.Pauli{0: pauli.Z, 1: pauli.Z}, 2),
    pauli.NewPauliString(0.5, map[int]pauli.Pauli{0: pauli.X}, 2),
})

res, _ := vqe.Run(ctx, vqe.Config{
    Hamiltonian: hamiltonian,
    Ansatz:      ansatz.RealAmplitudes(2, 2, ansatz.Linear),
    Optimizer:   optim.NewNelderMead(optim.NMConfig{MaxIter: 200}),
})
fmt.Printf("Ground state energy: %.4f\n", res.Energy)

// QAOA for MaxCut
edges := [][2]int{{"{"}}
    {0,1}, {1,2}, {2,3}, {3,0},
}
cost, _ := qaoa.MaxCutHamiltonian(edges, 4)

qaoaRes, _ := qaoa.Run(ctx, qaoa.Config{
    CostHamiltonian: cost,
    Layers:          2,
    Optimizer:       optim.NewSPSA(optim.SPSAConfig{MaxIter: 100}),
})
fmt.Println("Best cut:", qaoaRes.BestBitstring)

Exercises

  1. Vary θ from 0 to π. How does the measurement distribution change? At what θ is ⟨ZZ⟩ minimized?
  2. VQE is limited by "barren plateaus" — when gradients vanish in high-dimensional parameter spaces. Why might this happen?
  3. For the MaxCut problem on a square graph (4 nodes, 4 edges), what is the optimal cut size?
